## Folklore / unverified (NEVER ship)

- "1 second delay = 7% conversion drop": Kissmetrics infographic (2011), no primary study. The real 7% is Akamai's, for 100ms on smartphones. The "Amazon" version blurs a 2006 Greg Linden anecdote (~1%/100ms, never formally published). Our homepage strip of this stat was correct.
- "Complete GBP listings get 7x clicks / 70% more visits": attributed to "Google" with no findable Google publication.
- "Only 35% of SMBs have a GBP": single secondary synthesis.
- "Baymard + HubSpot 67.9% form abandonment, 4.2M submissions": likely fabricated content-mill stat; exists on neither primary site.
- HBR 2011 "within the hour" multiplier: real article, but secondary sources disagree by ~10x; pull the paywalled full text before ever quoting a number.
- RevenueHero 2024 response-time percentages, HubSpot "82% expect immediate response": primary pages not located.
- Gartner "25% search volume drop by 2026": real PREDICTION (Feb 2024) that did NOT materialize per 2026 retrospectives; only usable as "Gartner predicted, it didn't pan out."
- SearchMetrics brand-mention/AI-citation correlations: no traceable primary.

## Where competing designers drop the ball (ranked by recurrence)

1. Ghosting mid-project or post-payment (Trustpilot clusters; "rescue" agencies exist for it)
2. Holding sites hostage: withheld domains/logins (real 2025 lawsuit, The Register 4/22/25)
3. No measurable results; vanity metrics (Clutch 2025: ~1 in 5 SMBs report a negative SEO-firm experience)
4. Looks good, doesn't convert (near-universal complaint)
5. Slow communication, deprioritized feeling (48h+ replies)
6. No follow-up automation after launch
7. Opaque cookie-cutter work, no account access
8. Retainers with no visible tie to sales, no client control

receipts (/process), "you own everything" guarantee, 30-day support, direct
line, case-study numbers policy, tools that measure instead of claim. Wave-2
copy can name these complaints explicitly; the free-build FAQ already
answers 2 and 4.
