## The ask shapes (never a pitch wall)

### Template A: the free tool result (lowest friction, most contacts)

Subject: ran your site through something I built

Hey {name},

I built a free scanner that tears down websites for the five leaks that
cost service businesses the most, and while testing it I ran {their site}.

A couple of things came back that you'd probably want to know about, and
one of them is a five-minute fix. Want me to send the report over?

No catch and nothing for sale in it. I'm just putting the tool through
real sites, and yours was one I actually care about.

Russ

### Template B: the free-build slot (qualified sphere only)

Subject: I owe the universe five websites

Hey {name},

Short version: I'm building out my case-study library this summer, so I'm
doing five complete website builds for free. Real builds, same standard my
paying clients get: a one-page conversion site plus one automation, like
instant replies to every lead.

The trade is stated up front: a case study with real numbers, a public
review, and two introductions to people you think I should know.

You came to mind {honest reason}. If the timing's right, the details and
the application are here: https://generussdesign.com/free-build. And if
it's not for you but someone in your world fits, forward this to them and
that counts as one of the introductions.

Russ

### Template C: the referral ask (past clients)

Subject: quick one

Hey {name},

{Genuine one-line check-in referencing their project.}

Quick ask: I've got room for a couple of new builds this quarter, and the
best clients keep coming from people I've already worked with. If anyone
in your orbit is grumbling about their website or losing leads to slow
follow-up, an introduction would mean a lot. I'll take good care of them.

Either way, hope {business} is treating you well.

Russ
