Most business websites have the same problem. They look fine. They load reasonably fast. They have all the right pages. And nobody books a call.

The issue isn't design. It's structure.

## The Wrong Order

Most sites put services first, about second, and a contact form somewhere at the bottom. That's backwards. You're asking someone to care about what you offer before you've given them a reason to trust you.

The fix: lead with the problem your visitor already has. Show them you understand it. Then resolve it with your approach. Then show your work. Then ask.

## Speed Kills (Slowly)

Every 100ms of load time costs you conversions. Not theoretically - measurably. If your site takes 4 seconds to load on mobile, you've lost 25% of visitors before they see a single word.

The fix: static-first architecture. Ship zero JavaScript by default. Add interactivity only where it earns its kilobytes.

## Nobody Reads Your Copy

I don't mean they skim it. I mean the copy on most business websites is invisible. It's generic enough to belong to any company in your industry. "We deliver innovative solutions" means nothing. "We reduced client onboarding from 14 days to 3" means everything.

The fix: specific numbers, specific outcomes, specific stories. If you can swap your company name for a competitor's and the copy still works, start over.

## The Ask Comes Last

Your CTA should be the final step in a persuasion arc, not a banner that follows the visitor around the page. Build the case first. Earn the click.

Every section on your site should answer one question the visitor has, in the order they'd naturally ask it. When the last question is answered, the CTA writes itself.
