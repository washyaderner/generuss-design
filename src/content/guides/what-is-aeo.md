AEO, answer engine optimization, is the practice of structuring a page so an AI assistant can pull a direct answer out of it. Classic search optimization asks how do I rank in a list of links. Answer optimization asks a sharper question: when someone asks this out loud, can a machine quote my page and be confident it's right. The techniques aren't complicated, and most small business sites don't use them yet, which is exactly why doing so is an advantage right now.

## Lead with the answer

The single biggest lever is putting the answer first. State the question as a heading, then answer it in the very next sentence, before you explain, qualify, or tell the backstory. Assistants, and impatient humans, reward pages that don't bury the point. If your pricing page opens with your philosophy instead of a price, both a skimming customer and a summarizing machine give up before they reach the number. Answer first, elaborate second. Everything else in AEO is a variation on that one rule.

## Write headings the way customers ask

Headings are where a lot of the work happens. Write them in the customer's words, not the brochure's. How much does a bathroom remodel cost beats Our Pricing Philosophy. What areas do you serve beats Coverage. A heading that matches a real question gives an assistant a clean place to map an answer, and it gives a search engine an exact query to rank you for. The plainer the heading, the more useful it is, and the closer it sits to something a real person would type or say.

## FAQ markup and structured data

Some of AEO is invisible plumbing. FAQ markup is a small amount of code that labels your questions and answers so a machine reads them without guessing which text is the question and which is the reply. It doesn't change what a human sees on the page; it makes the meaning explicit to a crawler. The same idea applies to basic business schema that spells out your name, service, and location, and to lightweight files like [llms.txt](/guides/llms-txt-explained) that summarize your site for AI crawlers. You aren't tricking anyone. You're removing ambiguity, and assistants prefer sources that leave little to interpret.

## How AEO relates to classic SEO

AEO is a layer on top of SEO, not a replacement for it. Search optimization earns the visit; answer optimization makes the page quotable once it's found. Both sit on the same foundation: fast, well-structured pages with real content and consistent facts. If you've been doing the real SEO work, you're most of the way there, and AEO is the finishing pass that makes your effort legible to machines. ## A ten-minute test of your own page

You can audit a page yourself. Open it, read the first heading, and ask whether the sentence right below it answers that heading in plain words. Then do the same for every heading down the page. If you find yourself scrolling past setup, context, or a story to reach the actual answer, an assistant will give up in the same spot a customer does. Fix the ones that fail by moving the answer to the top of the section. This is the same thing a machine does when it decides whether your page is quotable, run by hand, and it usually surfaces two or three easy wins on any page you've had for a while.

## What a small service business should actually do

You don't need every tactic. You need three. First, take your five most common customer questions and turn each into a heading with the answer in the first sentence. Second, add FAQ and business schema so those answers are machine-readable. Third, keep your facts consistent with your Google profile and directory listings, so nothing you publish contradicts anything else. Do those and skip the rest until they're done.
