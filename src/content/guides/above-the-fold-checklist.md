Load your homepage on your phone, hand it to someone who doesn't know your business, and count to five. Then take it back and ask them two questions: what does this company do, and what were you supposed to click? If they hesitate on either, you found your leak, and it's the most expensive one on the page, because every single visitor passes through this screen.

## Why five seconds is the whole game

Visitors don't read homepages; they triage them. A stranger arrives with a problem and a back button, and the first screen either matches their problem or doesn't. This isn't about attention spans getting shorter. It's that your visitor has eight tabs open and yours has to earn its place in the comparison.

## The four elements that pass the test

**What you do, in the customer's words.** "Plumbing repair in Portland, same week" beats "Solutions for the modern home." The test of a headline is whether a customer could have typed it into Google. Clever headlines win awards; plain ones win calls.

**Who it's for.** One line that lets the right visitor feel recognized: service businesses, homeowners, event organizers, whoever it is. Specificity repels the wrong visitors, which is a feature. The wrong leads cost you time on calls that never close.

**One piece of proof.** A review snippet, a recognizable client, a real number you can back. Not five badges and a wall of logos; one believable thing. Strangers are running a quiet legitimacy check, and one verifiable signal passes it.

**One action.** A single obvious button: book, call, get the quote. Two competing calls to action halve each other. Everything else the visitor might want (pricing, examples, the story) lives one scroll down, not in the hero.

## What fails the test

Sliders that rotate your message away before it's read. Stock photos of handshakes that say nothing. Vague verbs like "elevate" and "empower" that could belong to any business in any industry. And walls of choices: six menu items, three buttons, a popup, and a chat widget all competing for the same five seconds.
