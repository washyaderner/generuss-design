If you are starting from nothing, the fastest path is also the simplest: do four things in order over your first month. Claim and complete your Google Business Profile, start a steady flow of reviews, fix your citations, then tighten your website basics. Do them in that sequence, and ignore everything else until they are done.

## Start with the profile

Your Google Business Profile is the highest-impact, lowest-cost move in all of local search, so it comes first. Claim it, verify it, and complete every field, with special care on your primary category, which influences your ranking more than any other single choice. Add your services, your hours, your service area, and real photos. A complete, accurate profile alone will lift you above every competitor who left theirs half-finished. The [Google Business Profile checklist](/guides/google-business-profile-checklist) covers each field in order of impact.

## Then start the reviews

With the profile live, begin collecting reviews immediately, because recency and steady flow matter as much as the total. You do not need a clever campaign. Ask every customer at the moment they are happy, and hand them a direct link so leaving a review takes one tap. Make the ask the last step of every job so it happens by system, not by memory. A handful of fresh reviews in your first month, answered promptly, starts building the prominence that local ranking rewards.

## Then fix the citations

Once the profile and reviews are in motion, spend an afternoon on citations: the listings of your name, address, and phone across the major platforms. The job is consistency, not volume. Make sure your details match exactly everywhere they appear, correct anything outdated, and claim the core platforms you are missing. This is largely a one-time fix, so do it once, do it right, and move on. [Which citations actually matter](/guides/local-citations-guide) keeps you from wasting time on the hundred that do not.

## Then the website basics

Only now does the website enter the picture, because the profile gets you found but the site is what turns a click into a call. You do not need a rebuild to start. Make sure the site loads fast on a phone, states plainly what you do and where you do it, and puts your phone number and a clear way to contact you within easy reach. A slow or vague site wastes the visibility the first three steps earned, which is why local search and a working website are one project, not two.

## What to ignore at the start

The fastest way to stall is to chase advanced tactics before the basics are done. For your first month, ignore blogging, ignore backlink building, and ignore the paid tools promising to automate your rankings. These are slow-paying, easy to do badly, and irrelevant while your profile is still incomplete. Most local service businesses never need them at all; doing the four fundamentals well is enough to compete in the great majority of local markets. Depth on the basics beats dabbling in the advanced.

## After the first month

Once the four fundamentals are in place, local SEO shifts from setup to habit. The profile needs its monthly touch of photos and posts. The reviews need to keep flowing, one per completed job, so recency never lapses. The citations need a glance a couple of times a year. The site earns the occasional improvement as you learn what customers ask before they call. None of this is heavy once the foundation exists; the hard part was the first month, and you already did it. Consistency from here is what compounds into a position competitors struggle to take from you.

That is the whole starting sequence: profile, reviews, citations, site, in that order, over about a month. The [local SEO pillar](/insights/local-seo) frames why this order works and how the pieces reinforce each other, so you can see the map before you walk it.
