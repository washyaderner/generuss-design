Your Google Business Profile is the highest-leverage asset in local search, and most businesses fill it out once, halfway, and never return. Here is the checklist in order of impact, starting with the field that moves rankings more than any other.

## Get the category right first

Your primary category tells Google what you are and which searches to enter you into. It is the biggest single lever on the profile, and it is the one people most often get lazy about. Choose the most specific category that matches your core service, not a broad umbrella. A "Mexican restaurant" beats "restaurant." An "emergency plumber" reaches searches a plain "plumber" misses. Then add secondary categories for your other real services, but pick the primary one as if your ranking depends on it, because it does.

One caution while you are in here: do not stuff your business name with keywords. Adding "Best Plumbing Repair" to a name that is really "Ace Plumbing" violates Google's guidelines and can get the profile suspended. The name field should hold your actual business name and nothing more. The place to be rich with keywords is the category, the services, and the description, never the name. Owners reach for the name because it feels like the obvious lever, and it is the one that gets them penalized.

## Services, attributes, and the details that match

Under your categories, list your actual services with plain names customers would type. Fill in every attribute Google offers that applies: hours, whether you offer online estimates, wheelchair access, payment types, service areas. These do two jobs at once. They help Google match you to more specific searches, and they answer the quiet questions a customer has before they call. Empty fields are missed matches. Complete the profile fully, once, and you pull ahead of every competitor who left half of it blank.

## Photos, on a cadence

Photos are not a one-time upload; they are a signal of an active business. Add a few real ones every couple of weeks: finished jobs, your team, your storefront or vehicle, the work in progress. A profile that keeps gaining fresh photos looks alive to both Google and the customer deciding whether you are real. A gallery frozen a year ago says the opposite. Skip the stock images entirely; customers can tell, and they trust the real thing.

## Questions and posts, the parts most skip

The questions and answers section can be seeded by you. Post the questions customers actually ask, then answer them clearly, so the first thing a stranger reads is accurate and in your words rather than a competitor's guess or silence. Google Business posts, the short updates you can publish to your profile, are ignored by most of your competition, which is exactly why a steady rhythm of them is worth the few minutes. Neither will single-handedly move your ranking, but both add to the prominence signal that does.

## The maintenance rhythm

The profile is not a set-and-forget page; it is a small, ongoing habit. A monthly pass to add photos, publish a post or two, answer new questions, and confirm your hours and services are still accurate keeps every signal fresh. That rhythm is what separates the businesses in the [map pack](/guides/how-to-rank-in-google-map-pack) from the ones stuck below it. Pair the active profile with a steady review flow, covered in [how to get more reviews without begging](/guides/get-more-google-reviews), and you have the two prominence levers that matter most.
