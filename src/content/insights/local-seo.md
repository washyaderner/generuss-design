When someone nearby searches for what you do, Google shows a map with three businesses before anything else. Those three get the calls. Getting in among them is not magic and not paid placement; it is checklist work most competitors never finish.

## The three assets, in order

**Your Business Profile.** Claimed, in the exact category customers search, complete on hours and services, with real photos added regularly. The category choice alone moves rankings more than most tactics. The [profile checklist](/guides/google-business-profile-checklist) covers every field that matters.

**Reviews with recency.** Total count matters, but recent velocity matters more: a business with steady new reviews outranks a bigger number that went quiet a year ago. Replying, including to the bad ones, is a ranking signal and a trust signal at once. [How to get reviews without begging](/guides/get-more-google-reviews) has the ask that works.

**Citations.** Your name, address, and phone, exactly consistent across Yelp, Facebook, Apple Maps, and the directories for your industry. Inconsistency reads as unreliability to the ranking system. [Which citations matter](/guides/local-citations-guide) saves you from the hundred that don't.

## Where your website fits

The profile gets you found; the site closes. A slow or vague site wastes map-pack visibility the same way it wastes ad spend, which is why local SEO and [conversion](/insights/why-websites-dont-convert) are one project, not two. Start with [where to start](/guides/local-seo-for-service-businesses) if you're at zero.
