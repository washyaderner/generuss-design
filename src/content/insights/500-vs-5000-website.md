Both numbers are real prices for real builds on this site, which makes this comparison unusually concrete: it's my own price list explained, tradeoffs included.

## What $500 buys, and doesn't

The [Launch Page](/paths#launch-page) is one page with one job: a stranger lands, understands you, believes you, and books. Hand-coded, fast on phones, wired to your calendar. What it doesn't do: rank for a spread of search terms, tell a deep brand story, or host content. It's the right buy when being credibly bookable is the current bottleneck, which for young service businesses it almost always is. [What a $500 website actually buys](/guides/500-dollar-website-what-you-get) itemizes it.

## What $4,500+ adds

The full build is an acquisition engine: pages matched to the searches customers actually make, local and AI visibility engineered in, and conversion attention on every step from first click to booked call. [What the bigger build buys](/guides/5000-dollar-website-what-you-get) itemizes that side, and [how much website your stage needs](/guides/website-investment-by-stage) maps price band to business stage honestly.

## The upgrade path is the point

The two aren't competitors; they're rungs. Launch Page money credits toward the Flagship build here precisely so starting small never wastes the spend. [Start small or go big](/guides/launch-page-vs-full-site) helps you pick the first rung, and [the upgrade path](/guides/upgrade-path-website) shows how the rungs connect through the Growth Engine.

## Let your numbers pick

The deciding input is what a booked client is worth to you. Run the [Missed-Lead Calculator](/tools/missed-lead-calculator) and the [Website Leak Scanner](/tools/website-leak-scanner); together they'll tell you whether you need the $500 fix, the $4,500 engine, or neither yet.
