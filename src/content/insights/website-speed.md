Speed is invisible when it's good and expensive when it's bad, and almost nobody measures it on the device that matters: a normal phone on a normal connection, not your office wifi.

## Why speed pays three times

First, humans: waiting is the one universal reason to abandon a page, and the visitors who leave don't complain, they just vanish. Second, rankings: search engines use page experience signals, so slow sites fight uphill for position. Third, paid traffic: if you run ads, every slow load is a click you paid for that never became a visit. The [honest math on speed and sales](/guides/website-speed-conversion) walks through how to reason about it without inflated statistics.

## Why small-business sites are slow

Nearly always some combination of: page-builder themes carrying features you'll never use, oversized images uploaded straight from a phone, a dozen third-party scripts each "only" adding a little, and cheap hosting far from your customers. [The six culprits](/guides/why-is-my-website-slow) breaks each one down, and [why page builders bloat](/guides/page-builder-bloat) explains the theme problem specifically.

## The standard I build to

Static-first architecture, no unnecessary JavaScript, images sized for the screens they render on. My own numbers are published with dates on the [process page](/process), because a designer who won't show you their speed receipts is asking you to take speed on faith.
