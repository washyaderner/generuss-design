"We get visitors but no calls" is the most common sentence in website post-mortems. The instinct is to buy more traffic. The math says fix conversion first: doubling your conversion rate is the same as doubling your ad budget, permanently, for free.

## The five failure modes

**Speed.** Visitors bounce before slow pages finish loading, and they do it silently. You never see the customers you lost at second four. The [speed pillar](/insights/website-speed) covers this in depth.

**Clarity.** Land on your homepage cold: can a stranger tell what you do, who you serve, and what to click, in five seconds? Most sites answer with a vague headline and a stock photo. The [five-second test guide](/guides/above-the-fold-checklist) shows what belongs above the fold.

**Trust.** No reviews visible, no faces, no real photos, claims with no receipts. Visitors don't call businesses they can't verify. [Trust signals](/guides/trust-signals-website) walks the fix list.

**Friction.** Seven-field forms, hidden phone numbers, no booking link. Every extra step sheds real buyers, and the ones you lose are the busy ones, who were the best leads.

**Silence.** The form works and then nothing happens for a day. Leads decay by the hour, which is why follow-up is its own pillar: [lead follow-up](/insights/lead-follow-up).
